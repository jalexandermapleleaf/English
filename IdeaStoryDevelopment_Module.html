Concept Explanation | 概念讲解

Interactions Between Individuals, Events, and Ideas

In nonfiction texts, nothing happens alone. Individuals (people), events (things that happen), and ideas (beliefs or arguments) are always connected to each other. When you read, ask yourself: How does one person's actions change another person? How does one event cause the next event? How does one idea support or challenge another idea?

These connections are called interactions. Understanding them helps you see the deeper meaning — not just what happened, but why it happened and what it means. Look for signal words: because, as a result, therefore, however, this led to, influenced by.

在非虚构类文本中，人物（individuals）、事件（events）和思想（ideas）之间总是相互联系的。理解它们的互动关系有助于我们深入理解文本的含义。寻找信号词：because（因为）、as a result（结果）、therefore（因此）、however（然而）。

Key Rules | 关键规则

  • Individuals → Events: A person's decision can start or stop an event.
  • Events → Individuals: Events change how people think, feel, or act.
  • Ideas → Ideas: One idea can support, challenge, or replace another.
  • Look for cause and effect relationships between all three elements.
  • Signal words help you identify interactions: because, therefore, as a result, however.

Analyzing Relationships in Informational Texts & How Elements of a Story Interact

In informational texts, you analyze how things connect — not just list facts. Ask: Does one event cause another? Does one person's idea influence a group? Does one event change the meaning of an earlier event?

In fiction, the key elements — character, setting, plot, and theme — push and pull on each other. The setting shapes what a character can do. A character's personality drives the plot forward. The conflict forces choices that reveal the theme. Removing any one element changes the entire story.

在信息性文本中，分析事物之间的联系方式。在小说中，人物（character）、环境（setting）、情节（plot）和主题（theme）相互作用：环境影响人物行为；人物性格推动情节；冲突迫使人物做决定，从而揭示主题。

Key Rules | 关键规则

  • Setting → Character: Time and place shape what characters can and cannot do.
  • Character → Plot: A character's choices move the story forward.
  • Conflict → Theme: How a character faces a problem reveals the story's message.
  • Plot → Character: Events force characters to grow or change.
  • Ask: "How does this element affect the others?" to find interactions.

The Elements of a Story

Every work of fiction is built from the same basic parts, called story elements. Recognizing them helps you analyze any story you read.

Character — the people or animals in the story. The main character is the protagonist. The one who creates conflict is the antagonist.

Setting — where and when the story takes place. It creates mood and affects character behavior.

Plot — the sequence of events: Exposition → Rising Action → Climax → Falling Action → Resolution.

Conflict — the central problem. It can be internal (inside a character's mind) or external (character vs. person, nature, or society).

Theme — the central message about life the author communicates through the story. It is a complete statement, not just a single word.

故事元素：人物（character）、环境（setting）、情节（plot）——故事开端→上升动作→高潮→下降动作→结局、冲突（conflict）——内部/外部、主题（theme）——作者传达的人生信息，是完整的陈述而非单个词语。

Key Rules | 关键规则

  • Protagonist: the main character the story follows.
  • Antagonist: the character or force creating the main conflict.
  • Internal conflict: character vs. their own thoughts or feelings.
  • External conflict: character vs. another person, nature, or society.
  • Theme ≠ Topic: "courage" is a topic; "Courage means acting despite fear" is a theme.
  • Plot structure: Exposition → Rising Action → Climax → Falling Action → Resolution.

The Elements of a Drama

Drama is a special kind of story written to be performed. It shares elements with fiction but has unique features.

Acts and Scenes — dramas are divided into acts (large sections) and scenes (smaller sections within acts), similar to chapters in a novel.

Dialogue — in drama, the story is told almost entirely through what characters say. There is no narrator.

Stage Directions — instructions written by the author (in italics or brackets) that tell performers how to act or move. Readers use them to visualize the scene.

Cast of Characters — a list of all characters, usually appearing at the beginning of the play.

Soliloquy / Aside — a soliloquy is when a character speaks private thoughts aloud while alone on stage. An aside is a brief comment spoken directly to the audience when other characters cannot "hear."

戏剧特有元素：幕（acts）和场（scenes）类似小说章节；对话（dialogue）取代叙述者；舞台指示（stage directions）——括号内的表演说明；人物表（cast of characters）；独白（soliloquy）——角色独自说出内心；旁白（aside）——说给观众听的短评。

Key Rules | 关键规则

  • Acts are large sections; scenes are smaller parts within an act.
  • Dialogue replaces narration — we learn everything from what characters say.
  • Stage directions are NOT spoken; they guide actors and help readers visualize.
  • Soliloquy: character is alone, speaking inner thoughts aloud.
  • Aside: brief comment to the audience — other characters cannot "hear" it.
  • Both stories and dramas share character, conflict, plot, and theme.
