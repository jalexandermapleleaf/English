Making Inferences | 推理判断

Reading between the lines | 读懂字里行间的意思
Core Concept | 核心概念

What is an Inference? | 什么是推理？

An inference is a logical conclusion you reach based on evidence from the text + your own knowledge and experience. Authors don't always tell us everything directly — we must read between the lines to understand the full meaning.

推理是基于文本证据+你自己的知识和经验得出的逻辑结论。作者不总是直接告诉我们一切——我们必须读懂字里行间的意思才能理解完整含义。
🔑 Text Evidence + Background Knowledge = Inference
Example: "Mia's hands shook as she opened the envelope. She took a deep breath and closed her eyes."
Inference: Mia is nervous or anxious. We know this because shaking hands and closing eyes are signs of nervousness.
文本证据 + 背景知识 = 推理。例子："米亚打开信封时手在发抖。她深吸一口气，闭上眼睛。"推理：米亚紧张或焦虑。我们知道这是因为手抖和闭眼是紧张的迹象。
Inference vs. Observation | 推理 vs. 观察

What's the difference? | 有什么区别？

Observation: Something you can see directly in the text. Inference: Something you figure out using clues. Good readers make inferences about characters' feelings, motivations, relationships, and what will happen next.

观察：你可以在文本中直接看到的东西。推理：你利用线索推断出的东西。好的读者会对角色的感受、动机、关系和接下来会发生什么进行推理。
🔑 Ask yourself: "What does the text show me? What does that tell me about what's really happening?"
问自己："文本给我展示了什么？这告诉我关于真正发生的事情的什么信息？"

Figurative Language | 修辞语言

Simile, Metaphor, Personification & more | 明喻、暗喻、拟人等
Core Concept | 核心概念

What is Figurative Language? | 什么是修辞语言？

Figurative language uses words in non-literal ways to create vivid images, emotions, and deeper meaning. Instead of saying something directly, authors use comparisons and imagery to help readers see, feel, and understand more powerfully.

修辞语言以非字面的方式使用词语，创造生动的图像、情感和更深层的意义。作者不是直接说出某事，而是使用比较和意象来帮助读者更有力地看到、感受和理解。
🔑 Literal vs. Figurative: Literal: "The rain was heavy." Figurative: "The rain was a waterfall crashing from the sky." The figurative version helps you experience the rain.
字面 vs. 修辞："雨下得很大。" vs. "雨像瀑布一样从天空倾泻而下。"
Types of Figurative Language | 修辞语言类型

Four Common Types | 四种常见类型

Simile (明喻): Compares using "like" or "as" — "Her smile was like sunshine."
Metaphor (暗喻): Direct comparison — "Her smile was sunshine."
Personification (拟人): Gives human qualities to objects — "The sun smiled down."
Hyperbole (夸张): Exaggeration for effect — "I've told you a million times."

明喻：使用"像"或"如"进行比较。暗喻：直接比较。拟人：赋予物体人的特质。夸张：为效果而夸张。
🔑 Why authors use it: Figurative language creates emotion, builds imagery, and helps readers connect to characters and ideas on a deeper level.
作者为什么使用它：修辞语言创造情感，建立意象，帮助读者在更深层次上连接角色和思想。
