The Outsiders, Ch.1 — S.E. Hinton (excerpt) 点击高亮词语查看提示 👆

When I stepped out into the bright sunlight from the darkness of the movie house, I had only two things on my mind: Paul Newman and a ride home. I was wishing I looked like Paul Newman — he's not even good looking, but he has this toughness about him. I had a long walk home and no company, but I usually lone it anyway, for safety. Automatically my hand reached for where my back pocket should be — sometimes I carry a book in my back pocket.

当我走出电影院，从黑暗走进明亮的阳光时，我脑子里只想着两件事：保罗·纽曼和回家的车。我希望自己长得像保罗·纽曼——他其实也不帅，但他有一种强硬的气质。回家的路很长，也没人陪我，但我通常独自走，这样更安全。我下意识地摸了摸裤子后袋——有时我会在后袋里装一本书。

I liked that part. I'm not like most greasers — I read a lot and even liked school, which was unusual for a greaser. Darry was always harping on me about it — "Stay in school, Ponyboy. We need you to get a scholarship." I'd rather drop out anyway; school bored me to death sometimes. My older brother Soda, the middle one, never cracked a book unless it was absolutely necessary.

我喜欢这一点。我和大多数"油脂党"不一样——我爱读书，甚至喜欢上学，这对油脂党来说很少见。达里总是唠叨我："好好上学，波尼男孩。我们需要你获得奖学金。"其实我宁愿退学；上学有时让我无聊透了。我的哥哥苏打，排行老二，除非绝对必要，否则从不看书。

I was almost home. It was a nice night — too nice to have anything go wrong. I had a strange feeling that something bad was going to happen. I just shrugged it off. We're in a dangerous position, us greasers. We're poorer than the Socs and the middle class. I could feel eyes on me — the prickling feeling on the back of my neck. It wasn't too long before a red Corvair was moving slowly beside me.

我快到家了。这是个好夜晚——好得不像是会出什么事的那种夜晚。但我有种奇怪的预感，感觉要有什么不好的事情发生。我耸耸肩，没放在心上。我们油脂党的处境很危险。我们比索克斯和中产阶级都穷。我能感觉到有人在盯着我看——脖子后面有种刺痒的感觉。没过多久，一辆红色科威尔跑车缓缓驶到我旁边。

"Hey, greaser," one of them said. They were Socs — the jet set, the West-side rich kids. It's like they got nothing to do but make it hard for guys like us.

"嘿，油腻鬼，"其中一个说。他们是索克斯——那群有钱的西区富家子弟。他们似乎没什么别的事可做，就专门给我们这样的人制造麻烦。
